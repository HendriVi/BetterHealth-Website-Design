Scientific and clinical foundation

Mental health is produced inside a living system.

The brain, body, relationships, learning history and environment continuously influence one another. BetterHealth uses this interaction to build a case formulation that is more specific than a diagnosis alone.

Deep phenotyping

We begin with diagnosis, symptoms and safety, then examine the functional domains beneath them: reward, threat, cognition, arousal, sleep, social functioning, autonomic regulation, metabolism, immune signaling, endocrine function and energy availability.

The aim is not to test everything. It is to identify which systems may explain the individual pattern and which measurements can meaningfully change treatment.

The systems we connect

Perception and prediction. The brain interprets bodily signals and the world through prior learning, context and expectations about threat, safety, effort and control.

Allostasis and autonomic regulation. Stress involves coordinated changes across autonomic, endocrine, immune, metabolic and behavioral systems. Its meaning comes from the pattern and the capacity to recover.

Sleep, metabolism and cellular energy. Sleep timing, glucose regulation, inflammation, cardiorespiratory capacity and mitochondrial signaling can change attention, mood, fatigue and stress tolerance, but must always be interpreted clinically.

Psychological and social systems. Trauma, attachment, coping, avoidance, relationships, occupational load and the person’s own meaning-making remain part of the same formulation rather than being separated from biology.

Evidence with restraint. Biomarkers and advanced technologies support hypotheses; they do not replace psychiatric diagnosis or establish certainty where the science remains preliminary.
