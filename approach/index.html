Scientific foundation

Why symptoms require an integrated view.

Mental health symptoms rarely emerge from a single cause. They arise from interactions among brain networks, metabolic state, sleep quality, inflammatory signaling, cardiovascular function, and lifetime history. Effective treatment addresses the relevant factors in your individual case.

Biological systems

The systems we examine include predictive processing and threat learning, autonomic regulation, sleep and circadian timing, glucose metabolism, cellular energy production, inflammatory and immune signaling, cardiovascular function, and physical capacity.

We do not assume that every patient has every abnormality. Instead, we measure selectively to build a coherent understanding of your particular presentation.

Core principles

Measurement guides treatment. We test when results could affect care. We avoid unnecessary testing.

Biomarkers require context. An abnormal laboratory value is interpreted within your clinical history, current medications, sleep, exercise, and other factors. A normal result does not invalidate your symptoms.

Psychiatry and biology are not separate. Psychiatric symptoms, psychological factors, and physiological states influence one another. Our treatment integrates all three.

Limitations are explicit. Measurement has limitations. Treatment does not guarantee improvement. More data is not always better. We distinguish uncertainty from certainty.
