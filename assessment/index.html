How we assess

Every measurement begins with a clinical question.

BetterHealth starts with diagnosis, risk, function and chronology. Deep phenotyping then describes the mechanisms beneath the syndrome: how threat, reward, sleep, physiology, metabolism, cognition and context are behaving in this person. Tests are selected only when the result can identify a medical contributor, refine a hypothesis, establish a baseline or change treatment.

Assessment domains

Clinical & psychiatric

We reconstruct onset, course, symptom clusters, impairment, risk, medication response, developmental history, trauma, relationships and current demands. This establishes the diagnosis and the pattern that every later measurement must explain.

Psychological functions

We examine threat learning, reward, attention, avoidance, emotion regulation, attachment, mentalization and coping. These processes help explain why the same bodily signal or situation can acquire a different meaning for different people.

Sleep & autonomic regulation

Sleep timing and continuity, breathing, heart-rate and blood-pressure patterns, orthostatic symptoms and recovery show how arousal is regulated across the day, during sleep and under demand.

Metabolic, immune & endocrine

Glucose and lipid regulation, inflammatory signals, thyroid and sex hormones, nutritional status and medication effects can alter energy, cognition, mood and recovery. Results provide physiological context and are not psychiatric diagnoses.

Energy & physical capacity

Activity, cardiorespiratory fitness, body composition and recovery show what the body can supply under demand. Selected indicators of mitochondrial function are considered when the clinical question justifies them.

Cognition, neurophysiology & imaging

Objective testing separates memory, attention, processing speed and executive function. EEG, qEEG, imaging or specialist assessment are added only when they can answer a defined neurological or treatment question.
