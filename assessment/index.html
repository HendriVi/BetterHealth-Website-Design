How we assess

Diagnosis first. Deep phenotyping next.

We begin with psychiatric diagnosis, risk, function and history. We then investigate the systems beneath the symptom pattern and select measurements only when they can clarify a hypothesis or change treatment.

Assessment domains

Clinical & psychiatric

Symptoms, chronology, diagnosis, functional impairment, risk, medication response, developmental history, trauma, relationships and current demands.

Psychological functions

Threat, reward, attention, affect regulation, avoidance, attachment, mentalization, coping and learned predictions.

Sleep & autonomic regulation

Sleep timing, continuity, breathing, recovery, heart-rate and blood-pressure patterns, orthostatic symptoms, arousal and stress tolerance.

Metabolic, immune & endocrine

Glucose regulation, lipids, inflammatory signals, thyroid and hormonal function, nutritional status and medication-related effects.

Energy & physical capacity

Cardiorespiratory fitness, activity, body composition, recovery and selected indicators of mitochondrial and cellular-energy processes.

Cognition, neurophysiology & imaging

Attention, memory, processing speed and executive function, with EEG, qEEG, imaging or specialist diagnostics considered only when clinically indicated.
