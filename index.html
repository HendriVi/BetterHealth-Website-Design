Beyond the diagnosis

Diagnosis defines the syndrome, the immediate risks and the treatments supported by evidence. The next question is more individual: why did this pattern develop, what is maintaining it, and which part is most likely to change?

We examine learning and threat, sleep, autonomic regulation, metabolism, immune and endocrine function, cognition, physical capacity, medication effects, relationships and life context. A measurement is added only when its result can alter the interpretation or the plan.

The BetterHealth method

A clinical explanation that can be tested and revised.

The process moves from the history to ranked hypotheses, then to selective measurement and treatment. The plan changes when the response no longer supports the original explanation.

01 / Pattern

Reconstruct what changed

We trace onset, sequence, triggers, previous treatment, sleep, medication effects, trauma, cognition, physical health and the point at which function or recovery changed.

02 / Hypotheses

Identify what may be maintaining it

The leading explanations may involve learned threat, sleep disruption, autonomic activation, endocrine or metabolic change, inflammation, medication effects, post-concussive change, relationships or another identifiable constraint.

03 / Measurement

Test only what can change care

A test should identify a medical contributor, strengthen or weaken a hypothesis, establish a useful baseline or alter the next treatment decision.

04 / Revision

Treat, review and update

Each intervention has a clinical target and a way to judge response. Symptoms, daily function, physiology and the patient's experience are reviewed together, and the explanation changes when the evidence changes.
