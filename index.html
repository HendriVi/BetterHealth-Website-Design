The clinical formulation

Symptoms describe the part of the problem a person can feel. A clinical formulation asks how the pattern developed, what is maintaining it and which part of the system is most likely to change.

Diagnosis remains the starting point because risk and treatment require structure. We then examine learning, sleep, autonomic regulation, metabolism, immune and endocrine function, cognition, physical capacity, relationships and life context. Measurement is added only when the result can change a decision.

The BetterHealth method

From diagnosis to a testable explanation.

Each step narrows the question. We reconstruct the pattern, rank possible mechanisms, measure selectively and change the plan when the response does not support the original hypothesis.

01 / Pattern

Reconstruct what changed

We trace onset, sequence, triggers, treatment response, sleep, medication effects, trauma, cognition, physical health and the point at which function or recovery changed.

02 / Hypothesis

Rank the mechanisms

We ask whether the pattern is being maintained primarily by learned threat, sleep disruption, autonomic activation, endocrine or metabolic change, inflammation, medication effects, post-concussive change, relationships or another identifiable constraint.

03 / Measurement

Test with a reason

A test is selected only when its result can strengthen or weaken a hypothesis, identify a medical problem, establish a baseline or change treatment.

04 / Revision

Treat and update

Each intervention has a clinical target and a way to judge response. Symptoms, function, physiology and the patient's experience are reviewed together, and the formulation changes when the evidence changes.
