The clinical map

Symptoms show us where a person is suffering. Biology, psychology and life history help explain why the suffering began and what is keeping it active.

We keep the psychiatric diagnosis because safety and medicine require structure. Then we go further by asking which functional, biological, psychological and social systems are most relevant in this individual case.

The BetterHealth method

From symptom to testable clinical hypothesis.

The plan follows a defined sequence and changes when the data, symptoms and lived experience do not move together.

01 / Pattern

Map what changed

We examine chronology, function, exposures, sleep, medication history, trauma, cognition and the exact way symptoms behave over time.

02 / System

Rank the hypotheses

We consider sleep disruption, threat and prediction, metabolic burden, inflammation, endocrine change, post-concussive effects, relationships and reduced recovery capacity.

03 / Measure

Test what can change the decision

Clinical assessment, biomarkers, sleep, cognition, physiology and wearable data are selected only when they can clarify the case or alter the next step.

04 / Adapt

Treat, retest and revise

Every intervention has a target, a responsible clinician and a way to judge whether it is improving real-life function. The formulation evolves with the patient.
